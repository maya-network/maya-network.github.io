About Us

The Yarn Network connects women and minority entrepreneurs from around the world with backers mentors and investors who understand the power of ideas coming from diverse backgrounds in business. We find early stage startups and small businesses with strong teams and compelling stories, vet them for depth of industry experience, passion, and teamwork, and share their stories with expert audiences like seed funds, angel investors, and venture capitalists as well as with people like you, who want to learn from innovators across the world.

Our goal is to create a new startup ecosystem that redistributes investment away from tech hubs like Silicon Valley and into areas of the world without strong entrepreneurship ecosystems yet full of innovators.

WE BELIEVE IN GREAT IDEAS

In 2017, venture capital firms invested in 4,475 companies, which included almost 10,000 co-founders. Of those co-founders only 9 percent were women, 17 percent were Asian American, 2.4 percent were Middle Eastern, 1.9 percent were Latinx, and 1 percent was black. For minority groups to even be heard by the decision makers of the funding world, they need to work much harder to just get to the starting line.

At the same time, Investors know that diverse entrepreneurs are a good investment. They’ve at least all read the headlines of Harvard Business Review articles about how homogenous teams lead to poorer decision-making and worse investment outcomes than diverse teams do. Yet most VCs will tell you that prioritizing investments in women and multicultural entrepreneurs is not a firm-wide priority. Why? When the potential gains are so high?

One of the main problems is that investors don’t know where to look for diverse teams to invest in. Their vetting methods are outdated, their sourcing limited to the connections within their existing networks, which usually lack members of marginalized communities. That’s where the Yarn Network comes in.

We know that the abilities of a founder and management team are among the most important factors that drive investment decisions. Investors tend to regard strong teams even more highly than the product or technology the team is selling. Through a series of interview questions and short videos, The Yarn Network showcases the strengths and passion of inclusive teams around the world. In short, our platform is a no BS way to put your money where your mouth is.

Not all stories are told equally.

We want to even the playing score.
